Frequently Asked Questions

What types of commercial facilities do you coat?
We specialize in warehouses, distribution centers, manufacturing plants, auto shops and dealerships, commercial kitchens and food processing facilities, aviation hangars, machine shops, breweries, and any heavy commercial or industrial environment that demands a high-performance floor.
How long does installation take for a large commercial floor?
Installation time depends on the size of the surface being coated. Return to service is fast — our polyurea/polyaspartic systems cure in just 24 hours, so your facility is back up and running the next day. We also work off-hours, nights, and weekends to minimize disruption to your operations.
Can your floors handle forklift traffic and heavy machinery?
Yes — that's exactly what our systems are engineered for. Our polyurea base coats are 4x stronger than standard epoxy, with impact and abrasion ratings designed for heavy forklift traffic, pallet jacks, and industrial equipment. We specify the correct build thickness and topcoat for your load requirements.
Do your coatings meet food safety and NSF standards?
Yes. For commercial kitchens, food processing plants, and beverage facilities, we install NSF-rated, antimicrobial coating systems that meet USDA and FDA compliance requirements. These floors are seamless, easy to sanitize, and chemically resistant to cleaning agents.
What is the difference between polyurea and epoxy for commercial floors?
Polyurea is 4x stronger, more flexible, UV-stable, and cures 5x faster than epoxy. For commercial and industrial use, epoxy is generally inadequate — it yellows under UV, is brittle under impact loads, and takes 3–7 days to cure. Our polyurea/polyaspartic systems are the industry standard for demanding commercial environments.
How do you handle moisture issues in commercial concrete?
We test moisture vapor transmission (MVER) on every slab before coating. If elevated moisture is detected, we install a moisture-mitigation primer system as the first coat before applying the base coat and topcoat. This is critical in cold storage, below-grade spaces, and older industrial buildings.
What happens if my concrete has cracks, damage, or oil contamination?
We repair all cracks, pitting, and spalling during our standard prep phase. For oil-saturated concrete (common in auto shops and machine shops), we use specialized industrial degreasers and shot-blast the surface to achieve a clean, mechanically prepared profile. Proper prep is everything — we never skip it.
How much does commercial floor coating cost per square foot?
Commercial projects typically range from $4–$9 per square foot depending on surface condition, square footage, system specification, and site access. Larger jobs benefit from volume pricing. We provide free, itemized site estimates — contact us and we'll walk your facility at no charge.
What is your service area for commercial jobs?
We are headquartered in Grand Rapids, MI (49546) and serve commercial clients within a 100-mile radius as our standard zone. For larger industrial projects (10,000+ sq ft), we regularly travel up to 150 miles and beyond. Contact us to confirm coverage for your location.
Do you offer warranties on commercial installations?
Yes. Our commercial installations come with a written warranty against delamination and peeling. Warranty terms vary by system and project scope — we'll detail this in your proposal. Our prep standards and material quality mean warranty claims are extremely rare.
