The A1 Standard

Our 6-Phase Installation Process

We don't skip steps. Every commercial and industrial floor we install follows this exact process — because a shortcut on prep means a floor that fails under load.

Phase 01

Diamond Grinding & Surface Prep

Prep is the entire job. We run industrial planetary diamond grinders across your concrete to mechanically abrade the surface, open the pores, and remove laitance — the weak top layer that causes coatings to peel. No acid etching, no shortcuts. Industrial HEPA vacuums run simultaneously to keep your facility dust-free.

Diamond Tooling HEPA Dust Control CSP 2–3 Profile
Phase 01
Phase 02

Crack & Spall Repair

We never coat over damage. Every crack is chased and routed, then filled with a rapid-cure polyurea crack filler that cures harder than the concrete itself. Spalling, pitting, and surface defects are repaired with a cementitious patching compound — creating a seamless, structurally sound canvas before any coating touches the floor.

Crack Routing Polyurea Filler Spall Repair
Phase 02
Phase 03

Polyurea Base Coat Application

We apply a 100% solids Polyurea base coat — not epoxy. Polyurea is 4× stronger, cures in hours (not days), and bonds at a molecular level with the open concrete pores. This foundational layer is what gives the system its forklift-rated load capacity and resistance to delamination under thermal stress and heavy impact.

100% Solids Polyurea Forklift-Rated Thermal Stable
Phase 03
Phase 04

Full-Broadcast Flake System

Immediately after the base coat, we broadcast vinyl flake chips into the wet coating to full refusal — meaning 100% surface coverage with no gaps. This adds texture, visual depth, and significant additional thickness to the system. For industrial applications, we can adjust chip size and density to achieve specific anti-slip ratings or chemical resistance profiles.

100% Coverage Anti-Slip Texture Custom Blends
Phase 04
Phase 05

Scarification & Surface Prep for Topcoat

Once the base cures, we scrape the floor with a flat blade to knock down any sharp, vertically standing flakes. We then vacuum thoroughly to remove all loose material. This step is critical — it ensures the topcoat bonds evenly across the entire surface and prevents any texture irregularities that would compromise the final finish or collect contaminants.

Surface Scarification Industrial Vacuum Bond Prep
Phase 05
Phase 06

Polyaspartic Topcoat — The Shield

The final layer is a UV-stable, clear Polyaspartic topcoat — the most durable protective coating available for commercial concrete. It resists chemicals, oil, battery acid, cleaning agents, and heavy abrasion. It won't yellow under UV exposure, handles extreme temperature fluctuations, and delivers a high-gloss or matte finish depending on your operational requirements. Your floor is ready for traffic within hours.

UV-Stable Polyaspartic Chemical Resistant Ready in Hours Gloss or Matte
